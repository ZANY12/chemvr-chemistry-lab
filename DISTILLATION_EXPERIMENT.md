## Theory Behind Distillation

### **Principle:**
Distillation separates liquids based on differences in boiling points. The component with the lower boiling point vaporizes first, travels through the condenser, and is collected as distillate.

### **Boiling Points:**
- **Ethanol:** 78.4°C
- **Water:** 100°C
- **Difference:** 21.6°C (good for simple distillation)

### **Raoult's Law:**
```
P_total = P_A × X_A + P_B × X_B

Where:
P = Vapor pressure
X = Mole fraction
A, B = Components
```

### **Why It Works:**
1. Heat mixture
2. Lower boiling point component (ethanol) vaporizes first
3. Vapor travels to condenser
4. Condenser cools vapor back to liquid
5. Collect purified liquid

## Common Mistakes

### **1. Overheating**
❌ **Problem:** Heating too fast  
✅ **Solution:** Heat slowly and steadily  
📊 **AI Detection:** Temperature rise rate monitoring  

### **2. No Boiling Chips**
❌ **Problem:** Bumping and splashing  
✅ **Solution:** Always add boiling chips  
📊 **AI Detection:** Irregular temperature fluctuations  

### **3. Wrong Water Flow**
❌ **Problem:** Water flowing top to bottom  
✅ **Solution:** Water must flow bottom to top  
📊 **AI Detection:** Inefficient cooling detected  

### **4. Collecting Too Early**
❌ **Problem:** Collecting before reaching boiling point  
✅ **Solution:** Wait for temperature stabilization  
📊 **AI Detection:** Temperature not at target
